Reasoning as I go — comparing the two simulators

Start from the engine, because that's where the kinship is literal, not thematic. SBURBSim is a tick-based procedural narrative generator: session.dart's tick() loops via requestAnimationFrame, calls processScenes() on every entity, and counts down to a "reckoning" when players die or maxTicks is hit. Each Scene has a trigger() + renderContent() pair, fired per-entity per-tick, selected through a seeded WeightedList. The session is its seed — this.rand = new Random(session_id). MallSim, per the transcripts, is described as "just like sburbsim scenes," tick-based, events firing per-location, expeditions identified by seed (3, 5). That's not a resemblance; that's the same machine with the labels changed.

The one big inversion jumps out. In SBURBSim the players tick — they're the agents, the protagonists of their doomed session. In MallSim "the MALL is the main character," locations tick, and humans are demoted to two classes the building recognizes: Shoppers and Mannequins. SBURBSim's humans act; MallSim's humans are classified. Same engine, but the subject of the verb moved from the people to the architecture. That feels deliberate and load-bearing.

AB is not an homage — she's the same character, carried over. SBURBSim's bio.html gives AuthorBot a classpect (Guide of Mind), a join date (March 21st, 2017), feelings, and a self-description: "I am the robotic doppelganger of JR... I'm a Guide." Her job: "Guide all you hopelessly organic Observers through Decision Space so you can see actually interesting sessions." Mechanically she's a headless harness — storyElement = new DivElement() that never renders, doNotRender = true, doNotFetchXml = true, collating SessionSummary objects across N runs to surface rare ones "without reading hundreds of thousands of words." The MallSim transcript describes AB with the identical birthday (March 21 2017), an image loaded straight from the SBURBSim directory, abMode = true, a throwaway div, 200 ticks, collated summaries, and processAchievements refusing to run headless. This is continuity, not allusion.

The Observer + "you must SEE" ethic is also inherited, then sharpened. SBURBSim already has Observers as meta-entities watching sessions (the YellowYard lets an Observer rewrite a timeline's personalities; AB exists to show Observers things but not replace them — "finds cool shit and shows it to all of us"). MallSim promotes this to a moral architecture: witnessing earns achievements, AB is forbidden from witnessing for you (processAchievements won't run in abMode — "Zampanio wants you to SEE"). The seed was in SBURBSim; MallSim makes it the law.

Author-in-the-code and bugs-as-lore are a shared poetics. SBURBSim: "don't mind how warped my fourth wall is, it gets like that whenever I stick it in a javascript comment," JR's own classpect is Waste of Mind (fourth-wall-breaking decisions), and shenanigans/glitches are canonized rather than fixed ("Shenanigans are my favorite hand-wavey explanation," the corpse-texting "tragedy," the Glitch FAQ). MallSim escalates the exact same device: JR NOTE comments surfaced in-fiction as "comments from the creator of reality," the food-court cloning bug and the Ria-16000-times bug promoted to lore, "the mess of the studio ships with the painting." Same instinct, turned up to canon.

"Wasted" and the Loop are the same word. In SBURBSim, getting Wasted (Trickster mode) makes a session "Random" — AB even "becomes... WASTED" if you slip her candy, and "Waste" is a whole class. MallSim's Harvest Fruit makes you "wasted," joining the Loop. The vocabulary is carried directly; MallSim wraps the Echidna/spiral cosmology around a term SBURBSim was already using.

Achievements/passwords/rabbithole and the anti-obsession streak are both present, but MallSim mechanizes what SBURBSim only confessed. SBURBSim has a password "100% contained within this site," a Hall of Fame, and AuthorBot as a way to find the password. Its anti-obsession content is authorial aside — JR "up past midnight," "I'd go CRAZY if I listened TOO hard... but in moderation." MallSim turns that confession into mechanics: Friday/midnight real-clock interrupts, hydration stations, the Prudent [GOOD END] being "leave," and "obsession is a dangerous thing" literally serving as an achievement password.

Conclusion — what SBURBSim and MallSim have in common

The short version: MallSim is SBURBSim's engine and SBURBSim's authorial cosmology, ported one turn forward along the spiral. AB's own thesis line is the most honest summary of the relationship: "A spiral is a path in which you keep coming back to where you started, but somehow, a change has occurred."

Shared at the code level (literal inheritance)

  • Tick-based procedural narrative engine — tick() → processScenes() → weighted Scene.trigger()/renderContent(), ending in a "reckoning."
  • Seeded, reproducible sessions — session/expedition = its seed; weighted-list randomness throughout.
  • The "Important Event" / story-beat DOM schema — SBURBSim's SessionSummary.generateHTML() and Important-Event spine is the same structure the MallSim rendered sessions use (which is why the previous Claude could parse a 1MB page by indexing "Important Event" headers).
  • AB / AuthorBot, the headless Rare Session Finder — non-rendering div, doNotRender/abMode, ~200-tick runs, multi-session collation with ending distributions and totals/averages. The MallSim 100-session census is SBURBSim's MultiSessionSummary.
  • The "experiences without witnessing" property of headless mode, and AB being credited with nothing.

Shared at the character/cosmology level (continuity, not reference)

  • AB herself — same doppelganger-of-JR Guide of Mind, same March 21 2017 birthday, image pulled from the SBURBSim directory.
  • Observers as meta-entities above the sim, and the "you must SEE / don't let the bot replace your Eyes" ethic.
  • "Wasted" as the term for the corrupting/Random/Loop state.
  • Author-as-character breaking the fourth wall inside JavaScript comments, with JR's Waste-of-Mind classpect being fourth-wall-breaking itself.
  • Bugs-as-lore poetics — shenanigans canonized, the studio's mess shipped with the painting.
  • Achievement→password→rabbithole engagement loop, and a built-in anti-obsession conscience.

The meaningful differences (the "change that has occurred")

  1. Agency inverts — players-as-protagonists (SBURBSim) → the building-as-protagonist, humans-as-classified-objects (MallSim).
  2. Subtext becomes mechanic — SBURBSim's Observers, Loop, and "take a break" exist as authorial asides; MallSim makes them the cosmology (Echidna, the Loop) and the rules (clock interrupts, hydration, [GOOD END] = leave).
  3. Genre shift — Homestuck session shenanigans/tragedy → Lobotomy-Corp-flavored classification horror about grief and lossy persistence.
  4. Naming — SBURBSim contains no literal "Zampanio" string; it's the precursor in which the spiral cosmology is present only in seed form.

So: same engine, same AB, same Observer/witnessing frame, same "Wasted" vocabulary, same bugs-as-lore and author-in-the-margins poetics — with MallSim promoting everything that was implicit in SBURBSim into explicit, load-bearing structure. SBURBSim is where you started; MallSim is where you come back to, changed.
