Claude — reading & thinking aloud
My reasoning as I read

What it is, mechanically

"Rabbit Simulator: Every Ten Seconds" is a Ludum Dare 51 entry (theme: Every Ten Seconds) that simulates Rabbit, the real screen-sharing / watch-party service that died ~2019 — "right before the pandemic," i.e., right before its exact niche exploded. That tragic irony is the frame. You're dropped into a fake watch-party room (iWantToBelieve's Room, "4 watching") with a looping video and a live chat sidebar of four friends watching a Let's Play of Zampanio together.

The cast

From objects.js: iWantToBelieve / "Belief" (the host — a conspiracy-minded internet historian who found the video), uMad / "mad" (troll), mikuFan1997 (sweet anime fan obsessed with "Parker-senpai"), asinineAssasin / "AA" (pedantic skeptic). Two Randos pull icons from a sibling farragofiction work, TwoGayJokes/Stories/MurderGameIcons/ — and the hidden display:none chat baked into index.html (theEnd/theMatch: "Shouldn't you be asleep? It's late.") is from that same set.

The core mechanic

(index.js) Every ~10 seconds the video flashes an AI-melted "BE KIND: REWIND." If you rewind, the chat rewinds with you — and each rewind/loop increments timesLooped and swaps in a different story from a 5-element cycle. The player's compulsive re-watching is the engine of the horror. The author's header comment says it plainly: "if you rewind, the chat rewinds as well. they notice. you've caught them in a loop... in the console, truth is crooning to you. come. join. dig a little deeper. what could it hurt?" — and console.log("JR NOTE: Zampanio awaits.") fires on load.

The five loops

The author labels them in-code: "north is normal, east begins to crack, south we've lost mad, unknown we've lost AA, and in west Belief is alone."

  • North — normal. Belief recounts the Zampanio creepypasta: predates Pong; talk about it and you (or your words) vanish; a guy found a "copy" (really a fanwork), got his stream taken down, and was later found with a gunshot wound, non-responsive, terrified — now only saying "zampanio is a really fun game. you should play it." Glitches flash every 10s; AA debunks; they bond.
  • East — cracking. Same script, but Miku already "knows" a creepypasta she was never told ("could have sworn i'd never told you"), and uMad's lines get overwritten by the refrain on the 10-second beats. The infection enters through the theme's clock.
  • South — mad is gone. A husk spams the refrain every 10s (postProcessStories literally injects these). Survivors argue dream-vs-supernatural; AA refuses to believe even after admitting "you don't have a body anymore either." They realize they're looping but stay aware, and resolve to do something different. The escape clue: mad was the only one who posted an image — markup, "the code underneath."
  • Unknown — AA is gone too. Belief consoles a sobbing Miku by reframing the vanishings as "they got out / went for help" — a cope. The husks keep chanting.
  • West — Belief alone. Everyone else is gone/chanting. She watches footage of "US watching... without me," sees everyone happy without her, swears off digging ("no more digging into things, better left alone"), tells herself she'll be "free as a bird," then the loop ends: "wish me luck." She's next.

Tells in the filenames

The unused AllYourFriendsAreHappyWithoutYou.mp4 and alonebelief.mp3 name the emotional core, and the dev note seals it: "have I mentioned lately that this is a Lonely game?"

Claude — verdict
Conclusion

The shape of Rabbit Simulator

The story. Four friends reunite on a dying watch-party service to laugh at a creepypasta about Zampanio — safe, they think, because they're only watching, not playing. They're wrong. As you, the viewer, rewind (the title's "every ten seconds"), you trap them in a loop and drag the narrative downward through five stages, losing them one at a time to the same flattening refrain — "zampanio is a really fun game. you should play it" — until only lonely Belief is left, about to wink out too. The escape the characters glimpse (mad's "markup," "the code underneath," the console crooning "Zampanio awaits") is an instruction to you: the way deeper/out is to stop being a passive spectator and open the source — the very transgression that consumes them.

Plot threads

  1. The safe-spectator delusion ("we're only watching").
  2. The Zampanio creepypasta + the murdered/comatose streamer.
  3. The rewind-loop the viewer causes.
  4. The one-by-one consumption of the friend group.
  5. The "escape via markup / the code underneath" puzzle.
  6. An elegy for Rabbit / lost media and the internet-historian's doomed preservation instinct.
  7. Belief's terminal loneliness.

Three speculations on deeper meaning (ranked by my confidence)

  1. (Most likely) There is no safe spectatorship — the audience is complicit. "We're only watching" is the lie; engagement (rewinding, re-looking, reading the code) is what damns them and you. It re-aims Zampanio's core thesis ("the search is the game") squarely at the watcher/archivist, and doubles as an elegy for dead platforms and lost media.
  2. A loneliness / grief allegory. The loops peel friends away until you watch everyone be happy without you; the "they got out / went for help" reframe is the cope that masks loss. The author flags it as "a Lonely game"; reads as depression / being-left-behind.
  3. Escape-through-layer-ascension. The only way "out" (mad's path) is to stop being "just words on a page" and become markup / the code underneath — the player's devtools transgression is the narrative's transcendence. Same reality-layer collapse as the Discord's "this was never a game."

Claude — the connection
How RabbitSim relates to the Discord files

Two nodes of one organism

They're two nodes of the same work, related in layers:

1. Same infection, mutated. The Discord's line is "Zampanio is a very good game. You should play it." RabbitSim's is "zampanio is a really fun game. you should play it." Same meme, drifted — and that drift is the point: Zampanio survives only as copies/translations/fan-recreations that each diverge, so an off-by-a-few-words refrain is a fingerprint of a different branch.

2. Concentric distance, same verdict. The Discord cast are researchers/archivists (one step removed — most never played). RabbitSim's cast are spectators (two steps removed — watching someone else play a fanwork). Both prove the same thesis: there is no safe distance. Discord lurkers and sim-builders get consumed; RabbitSim makes "we're only watching" the explicit fatal delusion. The further out you stand, the more insidiously it takes you.

3. The maze is literally the same maze. The Discord's NotAMinotaur navigates in cardinal directions — "/NORTH/NORTH/EAST/SOUTH…," "To the NORTH is ThisIsNotAGame… To the SOUTH is JustTruth…," basement/attic. RabbitSim's five loops are named North, East, South, West, Unknown. Both render Zampanio as a directional labyrinth, and both insist "this was never a game."

4. "The code underneath" is the shared trap/escape. Discord: the wiki is the map, reading source is encouraged, people realize they're inside it. RabbitSim: the only way "out" is mad's markup — "the code underneath" — and the console croons "Zampanio awaits." Both demand the participant break the frame by reading the source. (That intended "deep" move is exactly what an LLM does to analyze either one.)

5. Same degradation grammar. In both, selfhood collapses into husks chanting the refrain; both use "shambling" (the ShamblingHorror ↔ "shambling husks of our former selves"); both euphemize the vanishing gently ("they got out," "I found my fate," "going for a walk"); and both pivot on one obsessive who pulls everyone in — jadedResearcher ↔ iWantToBelieve.

6. By the franchise's own logic, RabbitSim is Zampanio, not just about it. The Discord says Zampanio appears as a different game to everyone and has no original — only branches. RabbitSim is one branch wearing a "dead watch-party service" skin; the medium is the personalization. It reads exactly like an artifact the Discord researchers would dig up and post to #testimonies or #bread-crumbs — TunnelGhost already describes a Roblox "account" of Zampanio that "wasn't really a game… like visiting a museum," and the murdered-streamer rumor (Parker-senpai, "the person always behind him") is the same curse as the Discord's deleted servers and dead-account owner.

Bottom line: the Discord is the sprawling hub — a community standing around the maze cataloguing it; RabbitSim is a tight satellite vignette — one branch of the maze rendered as a haunted Rabbit room. Different genre skins, one organism, cross-linked on farragofiction.com.
