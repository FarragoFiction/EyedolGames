### 6. The Simulator's Architecture — What It Generates

SBURBSim.dart (18KB entry point) exports key systems: Session generation
(complete game instances with unique seeds), Players with classpects (12
classes × 20+ aspects = 240+ combinations) driven by an AIEngine, procedurally
generated Lands (Themes + Features, with Prospit/Derse moons), 13+ named Big
Bads spawning on specific triggers (GrimDark sessions, etc.), Fraymotifs
(procedural special moves like "Put Sauce on That Dance Burrito"), Strife
combat, carapace NPCs who can be crowned with rings, the Reckoning (final
boss), and the Aftermath (win/loss/scratch), with dead timelines tracked in
DeadSession.dart. A SceneRenderingEngine plays 50+ scene types as narrative
text. The entire system is deterministic per seed, making sessions reproducible
and shareable.

Summary, narrative style: Profane, self-referential, and deliberately
broken-fourth-wall. JR frames themselves as both the creator AND an in-game
observer ("The Observer") who can interfere with sessions via "YellowYards".
The Wranglers are both developers AND in-universe entities. Every changelog
doubles as character dialogue; every bug report is a player complaint in the
game world. The tone mixes Homestuck-style irreverence with genuine technical
depth, making the codebase itself a narrative artifact about the process of
making a narrative generator.
