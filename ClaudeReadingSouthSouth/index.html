What sort of webpage this is

This is the source for a "zero-player game" maze toy. The page renders procedurally generated mazes (MazeBuilder, 30x20) and an autonomous avatar called the Wanderer (real_eye.png) that traverses them using a wall-following algorithm (always-left if clockwise, always-right if counterclockwise). The visitor doesn't really play; they make essentially one choice via a traversal-mode dropdown, which then deletes itself. The three modes reskin the same entity into three characters: the Wanderer (clockwise), River (counterclockwise, a vast slime), and Parker/the Bullet (NEITHER, who burrows "south" and breaks the maze). Stickers spawn inline with procedurally generated lore blurbs and restaurant names that link out to other "Eyedol Games" pages.

Emotional beats

The dominant register is melancholy about scale and confinement. River's collection is the emotional core: an immense being curating ~25 antique museum objects (fossils, mourning portraits, a "sad iron," counterfeit Egyptian busts), each annotated with grief about being trapped in a 50-year time loop (1972–2022), about deep time, about drones dying and resetting, about being too vast to fit into her own memories. There's body horror around Yongki and Pulcinella. The South/Truth monologue is a quieter, wounded plea from an entity that resents being forgotten and asks the reader to remember it. The North and East endings are about compulsion, restraint, and the futility of "winning" — JR scolds the player for not stopping.

What sort of page this is

A "found document": Witherby's diary, rendered as a series of dated entries. The conceit is that the diary belongs to one character (Witherby/Neville's partner) but has been written in by others who found it — River apologizes for trespassing, Yongki cheerfully defaces it, and at least one entry (Rabbit's) is [REDACTED]/deleted. The dates are nonlinear and labeled by loop number ("Birthday Musings #246," #244, #242, #203), which quietly establishes that ~250 loops have passed and that the narrator's birthday is July 10th.

Who the cast actually is

The let's-play payoff: this is a Lobotomy Corporation / Project Moon–flavored premise. A team from a "Corporation" that managed "Abnormalities" got pulled into a looping universe and became the monsters they used to contain. The diarist is the Solemn — Witherby — whose power is taking confessions and granting forgiveness (the "Skull Within," a sin-eater). His family/team: Neville and Devona (the Twins; Devona is the meticulous one with a bird form and a "shotgun" promise), Camille (the Captain, cursed armor, drawn to kill, and — revealed late — the first monster), Ria (degrades without Hope, the bonfire), Parker (the Bullet, can't be contained — your maze avatar), and Vik ("best to say nothing of Vik"). River, Lee-Hunter, and Yongki are newer arrivals.

Emotional beats

This is far warmer and more human than the maze files, which is the point. The core is a found-family-of-monsters drama about love persisting through horror: Witherby's pride and self-isolation, the Ria/Camille forgiveness arc, the Camille entry's devastating turn (Devona's head "softly thudding to the carpet," and Witherby forging the lie that "it was the armor, always the armor" to let everyone stay complicit and at peace). The Snow-Queen ice that froze the world except Neville. Yongki's childlike innocence ("I can get anything I want") landing like a gut-punch against everyone else's guilt. The "secret timeline entry" is a tonal release valve — a comedic entry about Ria and Camille's, ah, brunch-table activities driving Witherby to move out — flagged in code as deliberately findable elsewhere.
