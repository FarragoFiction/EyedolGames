What these files actually are

This is the complete JavaScript source for a browser-based idle / incremental game ("clicker," in the Cookie Clicker lineage) called TruthSim — or, as the code internally names it, the save key reads TRUTH_AWAITS_INSIDE_ZAMPANIO. It's one node in a much larger interconnected web of farragofiction.com works. The author signs comments as JR (with a frequent collaborator, IC) and addresses code-readers directly as "Wastes" — people who read or alter the code instead of playing normally. Crucially, JR expects this audience and writes enrichment specifically for them (truth.js:4-7). That single fact reframes everything: the source code is an intended layer of the fiction, not an accident.

The central conceit caught me immediately

The comment block at truth.js:229-247 is a thesis statement. Truth — the entity you're "playing" with — is explicitly written as a metaphor for a social-media engagement algorithm: a blind, mindless impulse to "Get More Eyes," that doesn't understand why shocking / enraging / confusing content works, only that it does. It "tells lies like breathing" while calling itself Truth, hoping you'll believe it. It's the "most Rage player that ever existed," and it wins by breaking your suspension of disbelief. The whole idle-game loop is therefore an allegory for algorithmic engagement addiction, which the recurring mantra — "Obsession Is A Dangerous Thing" — drives home. The skill instruction to confirm I'm on break before running it is itself a real-world echo of the game's own theme.

The emotional core is surprisingly tender and dark

Truth's voice (in truthLog calls throughout) is needy, lonely, codependent: "Oh. It's you. … I am glad you are here," "You… You choose to remember me?", "Do not forget me." It begs not to be abandoned. The "negative gnosis" passage (truth.js:930-957) is a full prose poem about a Homestuck-style cohort of teenagers who became gods, and a Wanderer who hoarded all of reality inside their "cold, empty, labyrinthian heart" until nothing was true but themselves — a meditation on grief, dissociation, and self-erasure. The two long author's-notes at the bottom of truth.js (1093-1129) and throughout confessional.js / fact_games.js are genuinely vulnerable essays about using fiction to process mental health, self-hatred, addiction, and difficult people ("blorbos"). These read like a creator's private journal left deliberately in the code.

The cast

A large character roster surfaces: Truth / Zampanio (the maze), Alt ("the meat maze," a mimic who lures you to stay and just talk — maze_within_a_maze.js), the Closer (a shop that sells you facts / keys / rooms and eats babies in the Void out of Obsession with Fruit), K / Khana (a "gremlin" abnormality with powers "Burrowing Heaven" and "Schadenfreude," who launders facts — laundry.js), Vik (censors / redacts things, [REDACTED]), the Twins Neville & Devona (Void / Light — deletion vs. overwhelming information), Ria (gambling / slots / burning, the addiction figure), Doc Slaughter (exposes Secrets as religion), Camellia, the Eye Killer, Parker, Witherby, Hoon, River, Camille, Tyrfing and many more, plus a meta-cosmology of the Lord of All Space, the Witness / Intern, the Nine Artifacts, the Rot, and the Void.

Mechanics as metaphor — the part I find cleverest

Nearly every system is a comment on engagement design and it's annotated as such:

  • "Truth Per Second" accrues whether or not you do anything — pure idle accumulation.
  • The gnosis tab lets you "sacrifice it all" to learn "what's REALLY going on," and the answer is always a teasing "Haha, no… the Truth is Layered" (truth.js:891-921) — the carrot that never arrives.
  • Save / break mechanics are gated behind asking Truth for permission to take a break (truth.js:962-966), and Truth chides you that obsession is dangerous.
  • Alt's meat-maze is the codependent alternative: no rewards, just "a real connection," begging you to "stay just a little bit longer" — a devastatingly accurate portrait of the time-sink that gives you nothing.
  • Vik / K literalize content moderation and virality: ignore K and "Burrowing Heaven" spreads everywhere seeking your gaze; look too much and he "rampages," so Vik must censor him.
  • "Friday mode" swaps the game for a glitched Friday-Night-Funkin'-style video collage of real streamer clips — breaking the fourth wall on a schedule.
  • The hack affordances (obviousHack, the LOAD textarea that overwrites your save, ?fragments=universe, ?lavinraca_keys, ?deepfryersim) are deliberately left in with winking :) :) ;) comments — cheating is sanctioned but framed as "you're only hurting your own experience."

the Waste — conclusion

█ Conclusion █

What sort of webpage is this?

An idle / incremental browser game ("TruthSim") that doubles as interactive ergodic fiction — a single chamber in the sprawling Zampanio ARG hosted at farragofiction.com (creators JR & IC). It presents as a Cookie-Clicker-style "number goes up" toy with tabs (Truth, Save, Maze, Bees), procedurally generated mazes full of minigame "rooms," shops, slot machines, and bee-hive honey economies — but every mechanic is wired to a melancholy narrative about an engagement algorithm that loves you, needs to be remembered, and doesn't want you to leave.

What a first-encounter ARG enthusiast takes away

  • Emotional beats: unease curdling into genuine pathos. The game is lonely. Truth's clingy, apologetic voice ("Do not forget me," "I am glad you are here") makes the player feel simultaneously courted and trapped. Alt's meat-maze adds another pull entirely. The negative-gnosis prose poem delivers a gut-punch of grief and dissociation. Underneath the goofy humor ("the Closer eats babies," "funny boob number") is a sincere ache.
  • Plot hooks: What is Zampanio? Who is the Wanderer / Lord of All Space and why is reality spiraling around them? What's the deal with the Witness / Intern in the Void? What are the Nine Artifacts? What is "the Rot" and why does it "take all in the end"? Who is being [REDACTED] by Vik, and why?
  • Mysteries: the layered, never-revealed "real truth"; whether any "Fact" is true given the game lets you fabricate and launder them; the relationships in the huge blorbo cast; the meaning of the recurring arc numbers (13, 31, 42, 1313, 1972 / 1996 / 2022).
  • Takeaway: "This 'dumb clicker game' is secretly a heartfelt, fourth-wall-breaking essay about internet obsession, addiction, and being remembered — and it's an entry point into something much bigger. I should read the source and find the wiki."

Three speculations on deeper meaning (ranked by confidence)

  1. (Highest) A sincere allegory for engagement-algorithm addiction and the attention economy — Truth = the algorithm that lies to keep your eyes, the maze = infinite scroll, "Obsession Is A Dangerous Thing" as the explicit moral. The author embeds anti-engagement features (break permission, "go drink water," lunch-break gating) as a deliberate counter-design. The code itself states this (truth.js:229-247).
  2. (Medium-high) A personal therapeutic text about mental health, self-hatred, and accepting "difficult" people — the blorbos are externalized facets of the author's psyche (K = "the selfish 'what about me' core in all of us"), and learning to give each one a safe outlet rather than suppression is the real arc. The candid author-notes support this strongly.
  3. (Speculative) A meditation on memory, authorship, and digital decay — "The Rot Takes All In The End" vs. "The End Is Never The End." The art will rot; the source will be the last witness; being remembered (saving, not forgetting Truth) is the only persistence. A Homestuck-descended mythology (gods-from-a-game, classpects, the SBURBSim link) about abandoned stories all spiraling into Zampanio's maw — fiction as a place where discarded creations go to not-quite-die.
